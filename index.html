The Ahl as-Sunnat Wal Jama’at is the largest group of Muslims and the only group whose beliefs and teachings are truly in accordance with the Noble Qur’an and Sunnah of the Most Beloved Prophet (Salla Allahu alaihi wa sallam) and Swahabath

It is the Muslim’s duty to follow the truth and to join the victorious group, the people of Sunnah and Jama`ah (Ahl al-Sunnah wal-Jama`ah ) who are followers of the pious predecessors (al-salaf al-salih Manhaj al-salaf and ahl ul-sunnah wal-jamaa'ah ), loving them for the sake of Allah whether they are in his own country or elsewhere, and cooperating with them in righteousness.
Characteristics of Ahl al-Sunnah wal-Jama`ah From the hadiths quoted above, and other reports, we can derive the following characteristics of the victorious group : They follow the truth. They are described variously as “following the truth,” “following the commandments of Allah,” “following the true religion,” etc. All of these phrases indicate that they are adhering to the true religion with which Muhammad (peace and blessings be upon him) was sent. They obey the commandments of Allah, which means: They are distinguished from the rest of mankind by carrying the banner of Da`wah towards Allah. They undertake the mission of enjoining what is good and forbidding what is evil. They will be victorious until the Last Hour. The hadiths say that “they will be victorious until the decree of Allah comes,” “they victoriously support the truth,” or “they will be victorious over those who oppose them.” This victory includes: Being open and not hiding: they are well-known and prominent and have the upper hand. Adherence to true religion, righteousness, following Allah’s commands and fighting against His enemies in jihad Victory by defeating others They are patient and resist others with patience. “What is Ahlusunnah wal Jama?”

Answer: Ahlusunnah wal Jama refers to the traditionalist and orthodox branch of Islam that emphasizes following the teachings and practices of the Prophet Muhammad and his companions. It is characterized by its adherence to the Sunnah (the way of the Prophet) and consensus (ijma) among scholars.Four Schools of Thought:** Ahlussunnah recognizes four major schools of Islamic jurisprudence (Madhahib): Hanafi, Maliki, Shafi'i, and Hanbali. * **Emphasis on Tawheed:** They believe in the oneness of Allah (Tawheed), affirming that He is the sole creator, sustainer, and worthy of worship. * **Rejection of Bid'ah:** Ahlussunnah discourages and condemns innovations (Bid'ah) in religious matters that were not part of the Prophet's teachings. *

“what is the diffrence between Shia and Ahlul Sunnah Wal Jama'ah?”

Answer: The main difference between Shia and Ahlul Sunnah Wal Jama'ah lies in their understanding of leadership and succession after the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him). **Ahlul Sunnah Wal Jama'ah** believes that the Prophet Muhammad did not explicitly designate a successor, and the community (Sahabah) chose Abu Bakr as the first Caliph through consensus. They believe in the legitimacy of the first four Caliphs (Abu Bakr, Umar, Uthman, and Ali) and follow the consensus of the early generations of Muslims (Salaf). **Shia Muslims** believe that Ali ibn Abi Talib, the Prophet's cousin and son-in-law, was divinely appointed as the rightful successor. They believe that the Imams, descendants of Ali, are divinely appointed leaders who possess special knowledge and authority. Here are some key differences: **Leadership and Succession.Shia:** Believe in the divinely appointed Imams, starting with Ali ibn Abi Talib, as the rightful successors to the Prophet.
