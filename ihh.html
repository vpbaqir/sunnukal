Qadar: The Concept of Divine Destiny

Qadar (Arabic: قدر) refers to the concept of divine destiny in Islam, encompassing the idea that Allah has decreed everything that happens in the universe according to His prior knowledge and wisdom.

Belief in al-Qadar includes four key elements:

  1. The belief that Allah knows all things: Allah's knowledge encompasses everything from eternity to eternity, including His actions and the actions of His creations.

    Allah says: “Know you not that Allah knows all that is in the heaven and on the earth? Verily, it is (all) in the Book (Al-Lawh Al-Mahfuz). Verily, that is easy for Allah.” [al-Hajj 22:70]

  2. The belief that Allah has written decrees: Allah has recorded everything in al-Lawh al-Mahfuz (the Book of Decrees).

    As narrated in Sahih Muslim (2653): “Allah wrote down the decrees of creation fifty thousand years before He created the heavens and the earth.”

  3. The belief in the will of Allah: Everything that happens is by the will of Allah, whether concerning His actions or those of His creations.

    Allah states: “And your Lord creates whatsoever He wills and chooses.” [al-Qasas 28:68]

  4. The belief that all things are created by Allah: Everything that occurs is created by Allah, including its essence, attributes, and movements.

    As mentioned: “To whomsoever among you who wills to walk straight. And you cannot will unless (it be) that Allah wills...” [al-Takwir 81:28-29]

The entire universe is under the dominion of Allah, and nothing can happen without His knowledge and will. And Allah knows best.

Reference: Risalat Sharh Usul al-Iman by Shaykh Ibn ‘Uthaymin.
